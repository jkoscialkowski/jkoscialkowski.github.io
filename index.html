About Me

I am a data scientist, a qualified actuary and an entrepreneur, recently learning the ropes as an analytics/engineering manager. I come from Bydgoszcz, Poland, but have lived in Warsaw, also Poland, for a couple of years now. In my free time I enjoy, in the most general terms, learning to create things. Or learning just for the sake of it. This has got me dabbling in topics spanning software, music, cooking and coffee brewing, oenology and probably many more. Versatility is what I usually elect over specialization*.

I have been thinking about putting up a page like this for quite a while for two reasons:

  • to motivate myself to learn the barest minimum about front-end dev,
  • as a place to list books that I have recently read, which are at least remotely related to what I do professionally.

The latter stems from an asterisk I place next to my general preference for generalism. I sometimes choose to delve deep into a particular topic as there is no other way, in my opinion, to gain a proper understanding of some concept than to read a relevant book and spend a considerable amount of time thinking about it. Looking at you, blogpost-educated data scientists. Anyway, I keep a somewhat curated list of titles waiting to be read, so whenever I finally manage to go through one, I will post it here. I conceitedly assume that a visitor of this website might find my choices compelling. There will be reviews too, one day. Enjoy.
